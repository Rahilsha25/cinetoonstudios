Building LiveOps Friendly Unity Projects

LiveOps isn’t DLC—it's the nervous system of every free-to-play title we ship. Here’s the checklist we run before launch day.

Nov 18, 2025 8 min read

LiveOps Starts on Day Zero

Everything players may want to tweak post-launch—cosmetics, balance, even camera offsets—lives inside Addressables. UI atlases never sit in the base APK; they are delivered as hot-swappable content packs so marketing experiments don’t wait for a programmer.

  • Ship storefront art, seasonal skins, and UI layouts as remote bundles.
  • Version every Addressable group so rollbacks take minutes, not patches.
  • Keep bundle sizes lean to respect cellular downloads.

Remote Config Owns the Truth

ScriptableObjects provide safe defaults, but Firebase Remote Config decides what is live. Difficulty curves, economy multipliers, and limited-time quests all reference a shared “knobs” document so designers, producers, and marketers tweak the same source of truth.

Goal: flip experiments without redeploying. Every LiveOps toggle is documented with owner, guardrail values, and rollback notes.

Instrumentation Mirrors Design Pillars

Beyond DAU and retention, we chart:

  • Unlock cadence and difficulty spikes per cohort.
  • Failure moments tied to specific LiveOps pushes.
  • Percent of players engaging with optional loops or events.

If a quest drop-off appears within an hour of a push, the dashboard links to the responsible toggle so we can revert instantly.

Guard Rails & Recovery Plans

Every content push runs through a staging track with synthetic wallets. QA maintains a “rollback cookbook” that anyone on-call can follow without pinging a lead. LiveOps should feel agile, but only when the recovery plan is written first.
