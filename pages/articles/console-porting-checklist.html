Our Console Porting Checklist

Everything we tackle before a build touches an Xbox or PlayStation submission portal.

Aug 05, 2025 9 min read

Performance Comes First

We profile on dev kits—not PCs—using platform ETW captures. If frame time spikes beyond 16.6 ms we treat it like a blocker and hand the clip to the responsible discipline the same day.

  • GPU capture per scene with annotated bottlenecks.
  • Thermal and fan-noise tests on both performance and quality modes.
  • Controller vibration budgets reviewed with audio to avoid spikes.

Certification Swim Lanes

Presence, save handling, suspend/resume, and network messaging each get their own Trello lane. A QA owner signs off every checklist item with video proof so nothing “assumed done” sneaks through.

Patch Rehearsals

We simulate day-one patches including version numbers, slot reservations, and delta sizes. Practice submissions save weeks when a platform only grants a limited number of tries.

Double-check CDN propagation, entitlement flags, and save compatibility before the real submission. Rehearsals expose edge cases early.
